Our Story

The Email

It all began with an email that Abhijit painstakingly crafted, introducing himself to Nina. It was a masterpiece of prose. He spent paragraphs talking about electronic music, how international he is, and that he was working on his own startup. Because, as we all know, girls love a guy without a proper job.

When Nina saw the email in her inbox she thought it was from her brother (who happens to have the same name) and forgot all about it. Little did she know Abhijit was waiting at the other end, wearing out the refresh button in his email. After several weeks, he worked up the nerve to email her again, and this time she finally responded!

The Courtship

There was an immediate spark, and what followed was over a year of constant texting and hours talking on the phone. She was in Chicago and he in San Francisco, so they didn’t get to see each other very often. But in March 2012, Abhijit moved to New York for work, and as luck would have it Nina did the same a couple of months later. Finally they were in the same city, and their love blossomed like the mighty mother Ganges.

The Proposal

In January of 2015, having surreptitiously acquired an engagement ring, Abhijit set in motion a devious plan to surprise Nina into marrying him. First, he planted stories among her friends that he hadn’t even started ring shopping yet. Then, eyes full of promise, he asked Nina to take time off next month around Valentine's Day so they could go on a trip. His plan was to convince her that the proposal would happen then but surprise her with the ring much sooner. Meanwhile, Nina had already seen the poorly hidden ring box at his place once while tidying up (editor’s note: AKA rummaging through his stuff). So she didn’t buy his story, but decided to be a good sport and play along anyway.

Then one evening, Abhijit lured Nina to The Lake in Central Park. He had picked a beautiful night; there was nobody around, the lake had frozen over, and there was a full moon out. Just as Nina was beginning to wonder why he had brought her to a deserted park late at night in the freezing cold, he launched into a rendition of “You’re My Best Friend” by Queen. Despite weeks of practice, he got too nervous and messed up the lyrics, but she barely noticed because at that point she was completely giddy. Before she knew it, he was down on one knee with a ring in his hand. Lucky for him, she was surprised after all, and the rest is history!
